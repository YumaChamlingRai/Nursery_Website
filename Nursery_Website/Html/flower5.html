Lantana

Description

Lantana is a genus of flowering plants that includes over 150 species of shrubs and perennials. They are native to tropical regions of the Americas and Africa, and are widely cultivated as ornamental plants for their colorful flowers and ability to attract pollinators.Lantanas are known for their small, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, pink, and purple.

Lantanas can be grown as shrubs, ground covers, or container plants, and are often used in landscaping as border plants or in mixed beds. Some species of Lantana have become invasive in certain regions, so it is important to check with local authorities before planting them.
